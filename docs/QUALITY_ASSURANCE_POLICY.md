# Quality Assurance Policy (Example Document)

This document defines the example quality assurance standards used within the Aurelius Protocol Governance Framework. It is intended for demonstration, portfolio, and educational purposes only. It does not represent a real production‑grade quality assurance policy.

---

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Quality Assurance Policy is to ensure that all documents, contributions, and releases meet institutional standards of clarity, consistency, accuracy, and auditability.

---

## 2. Quality Principles

All content must adhere to the following principles:

- **Clarity** — Information must be easy to understand  
- **Consistency** — Formatting, terminology, and structure must be uniform  
- **Accuracy** — Content must be factually correct and aligned with governance  
- **Completeness** — No missing sections, placeholders, or incomplete ideas  
- **Auditability** — All changes must be traceable and properly documented  

---

## 3. Quality Requirements

All documents and contributions must meet these requirements:

### 1. Structural Requirements
- Use consistent headings and hierarchy  
- Follow established document templates  
- Maintain logical flow and organization  
- Avoid unnecessary complexity  

### 2. Formatting Requirements
- Use consistent spacing and indentation  
- Avoid mixed styles or inconsistent formatting  
- Ensure code blocks, lists, and tables are properly formatted  
- Use neutral and professional language  

### 3. Content Requirements
- No ambiguous or unclear statements  
- No contradictory information  
- No redundant or duplicated content  
- No broken links or missing references  

---

## 4. Quality Review Workflow

### Step 1 — Initial Review
The contributor verifies:
- Formatting consistency  
- Structural alignment  
- Terminology accuracy  
- Compliance with documentation standards  

### Step 2 — Peer Review
A reviewer checks:
- Clarity and readability  
- Logical flow  
- Accuracy of information  
- Completeness of content  

### Step 3 — Maintainer Review
A Core Maintainer validates:
- Alignment with governance  
- Compliance with version control standards  
- Absence of unauthorized changes  
- Overall document integrity  

### Step 4 — Approval or Revisions
- Approved content is merged  
- Revisions are requested when needed  
- Major issues require additional review  

---

## 5. Quality Metrics

Quality is evaluated using the following metrics:

- **Readability** — Clear, concise, and structured  
- **Consistency** — Uniform formatting and terminology  
- **Accuracy** — Verified and correct information  
- **Completeness** — No missing sections or placeholders  
- **Traceability** — All changes properly documented  

---

## 6. Prohibited Practices

The following practices are not allowed:

- Submitting unclear or incomplete content  
- Introducing inconsistent formatting  
- Using ambiguous or informal language  
- Adding unapproved structural changes  
- Bypassing review or quality checks  

---

## 7. Disclaimer

This Quality Assurance Policy is an **example framework** created for educational and portfolio purposes.  
It does not apply to any real protocol, blockchain, or production environment.
